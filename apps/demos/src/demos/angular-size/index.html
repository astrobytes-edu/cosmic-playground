What to notice

  • Nearer objects look larger even if they are smaller.
  • Different (size, distance) pairs can look the same.
Model notes
  • Angular diameter uses the exact formula: θ = 2 arctan(D / (2d)), with D and d in km.
  • For small angles, a useful approximation is θ (radians) ≈ D / d; in degrees, θ (deg) ≈ (180/π) · (D/d).
  • Units: internal distance/diameter are always km; display may switch between ° / ′ / ″.
  • This is a geometry model (no lensing, no atmospheric effects, no finite detector resolution).
