What to notice 2 bullets
  • Day length and sun angle change with date and hemisphere.
  • Tilt drives seasons even if distance stayed constant.
Model notes toy geometry
  • Declination uses a simplified geometry (toy model): $$\delta = \sin^{-1}(\sin\varepsilon\,\sin L)$$ where $\varepsilon$ is axial tilt and $L$ is treated as uniform in time (about $1^\circ$ accuracy vs ephemeris).
  • Earth–Sun distance uses a first-order eccentric model (not a Kepler solver): $$r \approx 1 - e\cos\theta$$ Perihelion is anchored near day 3 (Jan 3) with an uncertainty of about ±2 days.
  • Key idea: opposite hemispheres have opposite seasons; distance variations are small and not the main cause.
